<p><p>The preserved fresh flower market comprises various drying methods, each with distinct characteristics. Air drying involves hanging flowers upside down in a dry, dark space, resulting in a natural, rustic look. Freeze drying removes moisture while maintaining structure and color, producing vibrant, long-lasting flowers. Silica gel drying uses granular silica to absorb moisture, preserving the flower's shape and detail effectively. Other methods may include glycerin preservation or chemical treatments, offering innovative alternatives for maintaining floral beauty and longevity.</p></p>
